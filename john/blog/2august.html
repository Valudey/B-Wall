The Circus Torso

A limbless body found decomposing inside a trunk in left luggage office in Waterloo station couldn't be identified. But Sherlock Holmes never give his final word. He immediately recognized the signature of a famous hitman: the Golem.


The Golem was back to London, after all those years, then. His modus operandi is strangling. He squeezes the life out of his victims with his bare hands. He's extremely dangerous, a formidable hand to hand combatant and was easily able to overpower Sherlock and I in a short battle.


However cutting limbs isn't his area. Strangely enough, he had a complice, even if he preferes to work alone. But the question was who? That's what we thought at the beginning.


Afterwards, the body was identified as a foreign circus member. Its members have been cut in a strange way. It was as if they had been ripped off savagely. Intriguing questions still remained. And you all know Sherlock hates not knowing. He deduced it was the masterpiece of a Felidae, certainly controlled by its tamer. After this murder, the tamer would have hire the Golem to get rid of the corpse and find a story for the police.


We would never know why. The circus left the country a week ago with our chance to arrest the murderers.
