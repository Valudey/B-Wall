23rd January

The Napoleon of blackmail

I think you all know Charles Augustus Magnussen. He was a media mogul who owned several newspapers. His activities drew the attention by the parliamentary committee, who wanted to know what interest he had in these high officials, one of them being the prime minister. Another Official Secrets Act case... But I can tell some things.

Here is how it started... After Sherlock's return from the 'dead', Magnussen took an interest in him and decided to test his "pressure point" to see what can truly break him. Magnussen has his men kidnap me on Guy Fawkes night and place me into a bonfire. You all remembered The Empty Hearse incident. Sherlock didn't know it was Magnussen at this point.

Afterwards, Sherlock decided to launch an investigation into Magnussen, claiming him to be the one man on Earth who can turn his stomach. Sherlock explains to me that Magnussen was an extremely powerful newspaper tycoon who controls world governments through blackmail and stores all of his information in a secret vault in his country estate, Appledoor House. Aware that Sherlock was after him, Magnussen went to 221B Baker Street and threatened Sherlock and I, warning us to stay out of his way before urinating in our fireplace and leaving. Later, Sherlock broke into Magnussen's office, and found him at the mercy of an assassin. And then Sherlock got shot. It was like losing him, again... He's fine now, don't worry.

At the turning point it was revealed that the Appledoor Vaults were no more than Magnussen's mind palace. Knowing that whatever it was that he wanted to spread, he could spread through his own news channel, Magnussen pictured and stored away his blackmail material in his mind palace, then sent the hard copies somewhere else to be sent for if needed.


And finally, it's gone wrong...

You've all seen the news, I suppose...
Sorry I can't say anything else. Official Secrets Act...
