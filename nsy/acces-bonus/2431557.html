Case n°2431557

Inspector in charge: D.I. Hopkins


A gas explosion at a block of flats took place in Garratt Lane, Earlsfield (South-West London). One woman who lives close by told she thought the "enormous bang... might have been a bomb or a train crash" after it "rocked" her house.

It was believed the explosion happened on the ground floor of the building. The Health and Safety Executive had be informed in due course and the gas board was at the scene.

A gas explosion is an accident that occurs when the fuel-oxidant mixture is within the explosive limits in the air and the mixture is accidentally ignited (activation of heating, lightning ignition, alarm...) or intentional (suicide or assault).

Emergency services were called to Garratt Lane, Earlsfield, at 07:25 BST

No significant change have been found : activation of heating, lightning ignition, alarm...
It seems the explosion was intentional.

No witness. No suspect.
